Preface

Every computer provides the means to store data and to operate on data and attached devices. The operations are implemented with a fixed set of instructions. One of the ways computers can differ is in the details of their instruction set; however, the fundamental instructions of most modern computers are similar.

Our Model Computer is of the modern Reduced Instruction Set Computer (RISC) variety rather than an old fashioned Complex Instruction Set Computer (CISC). RISC machines have a relatively small set of instructions compared to CISC machines, and all of the instructions are simple. (Some of the instructions of CISC machines can be very complex.)

Complex tasks require RISC machines to execute a longer sequence of instructions than would be required of a CISC machine. However, the simplicity of RISC machines allows the electronic circuits which execute the instructions to execute them faster than the corresponding circuits achieve with CISC machines. The end result is that RISC machines tend to be faster than CISC machines on most tasks.

For us there is an additional benefit of the simplicity of RISC machines instructions: There is less to learn. In fact, I've reduced the complexity of the instruction set even a bit more for our Model Computer.
