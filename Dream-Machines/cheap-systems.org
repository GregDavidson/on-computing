* The Cost of Shoddy Commodity Computer Systems

Computers and software are everywhere today.  In a few decades they've become
- millions of times cheaper
- millions of times more powerful
- easier to use for many purposes

Unfortunatley, they're also
- insecure
- unreliable -- data is easily lost
- harder to use for innovative goals

With computers now so essential to our lives, including our finances,
it is no urgent that we at least solve the problem of their
insecurity.  This is difficult because
- Security can't be added to an insecure system.
- Security must be part of the fundamental design of a secure system.

Fortunately
- We long known how to build secure computer systems.
- Most of the key technical pieces are readily available.

Unfortunately
- Inertia 

The best approach will solve all three problems.
- Build at least one flexible but secure computer platform
  - Formally Verified Hardware
  - Formally Verified Operating System Components
  - Eschewing functionally unnecessary complexity
    - Non-Modifiable data where possible
    - Transactional monotonic major versioning where modification required
      and monotonicity is possible
    - Transactional non-monotonic major-versioning where modification
      is required and monotonicity is not possible
